🚨 Salmonella Alert! Protect Your Feathered Friend 🚨


Ever heard of salmonella? It's a nasty bacteria that can give your chicken a tummy ache. It lives in some foods and can make them feel yucky.


Signs your chicken might have salmonella:


  • 🐣 Poopy problems: Look for diarrhea, which is watery poop that's not a good sign.
  • 🐣 Sleepy chicken? Lethargy means your chicken has low energy and might not feel like playing.
  • 🐣 Picky eater? Loss of appetite can be a symptom of salmonella.
  • 🐣 Dehydration danger: If your chicken isn't drinking, they could get dehydrated, which is serious.

    • Don't worry, we can help prevent salmonella! Here's how to keep your chicken healthy:


  • 🐣 Cleanliness is key: Regularly clean and disinfect their coop, food bowls, and water dishes to keep germs away.
  • 🐣 Healthy food, happy chicken: Give them food that's safe and free of salmonella.
  • 🐣 Wash your hands! Wash your hands well after handling your chicken to stop the spread of bacteria.
